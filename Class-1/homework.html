strong vs b Tag :

strong Tag :

1. Used to give semantic importance to the text.

2. It indicates that the content has a strong emphasis or is of high importance.

3. Screen readers often read strong text with a different tone to convey its significance.

 Example:
Warning: This action cannot be undone.

b Tag :

1. Used purely for styling purposes (i.e., to make the text bold).

2. It does not convey any semantic meaning or importance to the text.

3. Itâ€™s mainly used when you want to draw attention to text without implying any extra emphasis.

 Example:
This is a bold statement.

Summary:
- Use strong when you want to emphasize the importance or urgency of the text.
- Use b when you just want to make text bold for visual styling.

i vs em Tag

em Tag:

1. Used to give semantic emphasis to the text.

2. Indicates that the text should be stressed or highlighted for emphasis.

3. creen readers often read em text with a different tone or emphasis.

 Example:
Don't forget to check your email.

i Tag:

1. Used purely for styling purposes (i.e., to make the text italic).

2. It does not convey any semantic meaning or emphasis.

3. Often used for technical terms, foreign words, or phrases, without any added stress.

 Example:
This is an italicized word.

Summary:
- Use em when you want to emphasize the text with added meaning or stress.
- Use i when you just want to make text italic for visual styling without any added emphasis.
